About me

Fourteen years, every link of the chain.

From assistant merchandiser on a factory floor in Mymensingh to leading merchandising teams for European retailers in Dhaka — and now, into the AI era of apparel.

My mission

Help garment manufacturing get smarter — without losing what makes it work.

“The next decade of RMG belongs to people who pair floor experience with technology. I intend to be one of them — and to bring others along.”

I started as an assistant merchandiser at Crystal Martin Apparel in 2011 — chasing lab dips, preparing costings, learning what a critical path means when the production floor is right downstairs. That grounding shaped everything since: I have never quoted a price I could not trace back to yarn, or promised a delivery date without knowing what the factory can actually run.

At New Wave Group, I was one of two merchandisers who started the group’s trading business through its intercompany, Multi Sourcing Asia Ltd. We built it from a standing start to USD 35 million in annual order value, opening 15 buyer accounts — Björn Borg, SEBAGO, Volvo AB, and Aditya Birla Fashion & Retail among them.

Five years at Celio followed: I independently ran the tee, polo, sweatshirt, nightwear, pyjama, and accessory accounts — 1.5 million pieces a year, 70+ styles a season across 14 factories, 94% on-time delivery — and helped push 30% of the collection into sustainable production.

Today I lead a four-person merchandising team at LPP S.A.’s Bangladesh liaison office: about 3 million pieces a year across 35 vendors, denim for Cropp, House, and Mohito, accessories for all five LPP brands — Sinsay, Reserved, Cropp, House, and Mohito. Last season we hit 97% on-time delivery and grew Dhaka office order volume 10% against target.

Alongside the day job, I study and I write. A Post Graduate Diploma in Garments Business at IBA (University of Dhaka), the AI for Professional Success (AiPS) capacity-building program, a weekly newsletter, daily posts. The industry is changing faster than most sourcing playbooks — I would rather write the new one than be surprised by it.
